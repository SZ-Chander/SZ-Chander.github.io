# The Last Day of My Part-Time Job
Today, February 28, 2026, was my last day working.  
  
It was a job that carried real responsibility. At the lightest level, it involved assisting passengers with their travel arrangements. At its heaviest, it could be related to their safety. And yet, strangely, I never felt overwhelmed by pressure. On the contrary, it was here that I found a sense of belonging.  
  
As an international student, there are many things I cannot do as perfectly as I would like. My Japanese is still far from native-level, even though the job requires constant communication. My English is not particularly strong either, despite regularly interacting with travelers from all over the world. Effort was the only thing I could truly offer.  
  
Fortunately, this place accepted me as I was. For the past year and a half, it has given me a feeling of having a home.  
  
Partings are always bittersweet. I never expected that some of my colleagues would almost tear up because I was leaving. Everyone was incredibly kind — they even treated me to a truly wonderful hamburger meal.  
  
Honestly, I have never thought of myself as being that special, nor did I ever imagine that they would care for me to such an extent.
